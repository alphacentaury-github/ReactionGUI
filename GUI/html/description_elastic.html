In general, the elastic scattering would be described with the optical model potential (OMP). Over the years, the standard form of the optical potential has evolved. This is \[U_\textrm{opt}(r) = V_\textrm{coul}(r) + U_\textrm{vol}(r) + U_\textrm{surf}(r) + (\frac{\hbar}{m_\pi c\large})^2~U_\textrm{so}(r)~\textbf{l}\cdot\textbf{s}~~\] and each \(U(r)\) could be divided by real and imaginary part.

If the appropriate optical potential parameters are known, you can enter those values directly in next [Potentials tab]. Here several global OMPs are proposed for the reaction of interest. These global OMPs are originated from RIPL-3 library. Alternatively the optical potential may be calculated using a folding model.
